Certificate Management is a premium feature for creating and digitally sealing certificates: diplomas, attestations, memberships, or any document issued under your authority. Each sealing operation consumes one Certificate Seal Credit.

## Two ways to create a certificate

<Columns cols={2}>
  <Card title="Upload your own PDF" icon="file-arrow-up">
    Upload a finished PDF, then position the QR code overlay anywhere on the
    document in the certificate editor before sealing.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Use a template" icon="table-layout">
    Pick a template, fill in its fields (certificate name, company name,
    dates), save, and seal the generated certificate.
  </Card>
</Columns>

In both cases you choose the regulation at sealing time: **eIDAS** (EU compliance) or **ZertES** (Swiss compliance). Status moves Draft, then In Review, then Sealed.

## Signature options

Beyond the plain seal, a certificate can carry personal signatures collected before sealing:

| Variant | What it adds |
|---|---|
| **Standard** | Plain seal, no personal signature |
| **e-Seal (QR seal)** | Organization e-seal with the QR verification code |
| **e-Signature (Mobile ID QES)** | Qualified electronic signatures via Swisscom Mobile ID; multiple signers each confirm on their phone |
| **e-Seal + e-Signature** | Both combined on one certificate |
| **Hand-sign + timestamp** | A drawn or uploaded visual signature plus the timestamp seal |
| **Hand-sign + Mobile ID QES** | A hand-drawn signature combined with a single-signer qualified signature |

<Note>
  Hand-signing is a visual signature that documents consent; it is not a
  qualified electronic signature on its own, which is why it is always
  combined with a seal or a QES.
</Note>

## Multi-signer certificates

- The owner can invite co-signers, including external people for hand-signing. Invited hand-signers receive a personal signing link and need no account.
- The owner can choose to sign **last**, after everyone else.
- Pending signers can be **revoked**, and their reminder emails can be stopped.
- Signing links are personal and expire. Once a certificate is sealed, no further signatures can be added.

When all signatures are in and sealing completes, the owner receives a real-time notification and the final signed PDF is emailed automatically.

## After sealing

- Sealed certificates show a green Sealed badge and can only be viewed or deleted, never edited
- The sealed PDF is available via the Download Certificate button
- Anyone can verify the result in the [Doc Validator](/sealing/verification)
