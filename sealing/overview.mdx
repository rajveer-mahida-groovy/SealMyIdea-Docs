Sealing applies a cryptographic, legally recognized seal to a PDF so that its content, and the time it existed, are provably fixed and tamper-evident. If even a single byte of a sealed document changes afterwards, verification fails.

## What a seal contains

When SealMyIdea seals a document, it produces:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Cryptographic hash">
    A SHA-256 digest of the document is computed. This fingerprint changes if
    the document changes in any way.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Qualified timestamp">
    A verified timestamp from Swisscom Trust Services fixes the exact moment
    the document existed, under ZertES or eIDAS rules.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Embedded digital signature">
    A PAdES signature is embedded directly in the PDF using the Swisscom AIS
    (All-in Signing Service), with long-term validation (LTV) data so the seal
    stays verifiable even after certificates expire.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Visual signature and QR code">
    A signature box and a QR code are stamped on the document. Scanning the QR
    code opens a public verification page for that exact document.
  </Step>
</Steps>

For folder sealing, you additionally get a ZIP package of the sealed files and a "PDF with proof" certificate summarizing the seal.

## Key properties

- **Irreversible**: once sealed, a document is permanently timestamped and cannot be altered. There is no "unseal".
- **Tamper-evident**: any modification after sealing makes the signature invalid, and the [Doc Validator](/sealing/verification) reports it.
- **Independently verifiable**: anyone can verify a sealed PDF via its QR code or the public validator, with no SealMyIdea account.
- **Legally grounded**: seals and signatures are issued through Swisscom Trust Services, a qualified trust service provider, under ZertES (Switzerland) and eIDAS (EU). See [Compliance](/sealing/compliance).

## Seal or signature?

The two serve different purposes and can be combined:

| | Proves | Who |
|---|---|---|
| **Seal (trusted timestamp / e-seal)** | The document existed at a point in time and has not changed since | The organization / platform |
| **Qualified electronic signature (QES)** | A specific person signed the document, with legal equivalence to a handwritten signature | An individual, approved on their phone via Swisscom Mobile ID |

Read [Seal types](/sealing/seal-types) for every variant SealMyIdea offers.

## Credits

Sealing operations consume credits, purchased in the dashboard under Settings:

- **Folder-sealing credits** for folder timestamp seals
- **eIDAS / ZertES e-Signature credits** for qualified electronic signatures (separate counters per regulation)
- **Certificate seal credits** for certificate sealing
- **UAE Pass e-Signature credits** for UAE Pass signatures

Agencies and tenants can share credit pools down their organization hierarchy.
