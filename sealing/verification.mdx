Every seal is designed to be verified by anyone, without a SealMyIdea account, subscription, or credits.

## QR code on the sealed PDF

Each sealed document carries a QR code in its visual signature box. Scanning it opens a public verification page for that exact document, resolved through a unique verification token. The page shows the seal details so a third party holding a printout can confirm the original.

The same check is available programmatically through the public verify endpoint (no authentication required); see the API reference.

## Doc Validator

The **Doc Validator** checks whether a PDF was sealed on SealMyIdea and whether its qualified signature is still valid. It supports **eIDAS**, **ZertES**, and **UAE Pass** signatures.

Drag and drop a PDF; validation runs automatically and shows:

- Document name, seal ID, and a signature-count summary
- A green **Valid** badge when the signature checks out
- Full signer certificate details: signature type, initial signature time, valid-until date, certificate subject, issuer, and serial number

A **Download report as PDF** button produces a formal validation report, useful for legal and compliance purposes.

## Validation statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Valid** | Signature intact; the document has not changed since sealing |
| **Invalid** | Signature failed verification; the document may have been altered |
| **No signature found** | The PDF was never sealed on SealMyIdea, or is not a sealed PDF |

## Why seals stay verifiable

Seals embed long-term validation (LTV) data alongside the PAdES signature: certificate chains and revocation information are stored inside the PDF itself. A sealed document therefore remains verifiable years later, even after the signing certificates expire.
