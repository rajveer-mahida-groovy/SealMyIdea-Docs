## Legal frameworks

SealMyIdea seals and signatures are issued under two electronic signature laws, selected per document:

| Regulation | Jurisdiction | What it governs |
|---|---|---|
| **ZertES** | Switzerland | The Swiss federal act on electronic signatures |
| **eIDAS** | European Union | The EU regulation on electronic identification and trust services |

For UAE-based signing, **UAE Pass** digital signatures are supported as a third option in the web app.

<Note>
  Regulation values in the API are case-sensitive: exactly `ZertES` or
  `eIDAS`. Lowercase values are rejected.
</Note>

## Qualified electronic signatures

A **qualified electronic signature (QES)** is the strongest signature level under both frameworks and is legally equivalent to a handwritten signature in the respective jurisdiction. SealMyIdea issues QES through **Swisscom Trust Services**, a qualified trust service provider (QTSP), using:

- **Swisscom Mobile ID** for registered signers
- The **on-demand flow** (ETSI-standard identification with a verified mobile number) for signers without prior registration
- **UAE Pass** for UAE-based signers

## Technical standards

- **PAdES** (PDF Advanced Electronic Signatures): signatures are embedded directly in the PDF. UAE Pass e-seals also support **CAdES**.
- **SHA-256** digests for document hashing.
- **Qualified timestamps** from Swisscom Trust Services fix the sealing time.
- **LTV (long-term validation)**: certificate chains and revocation data are embedded in the sealed PDF, so verification keeps working after certificates expire.
- **ETSI** standards govern the on-demand QES identification and signing flow.

## What this means in practice

- A timestamp seal proves integrity and existence at a point in time, backed by a qualified timestamp
- A QES binds a specific, identified person to the document with handwritten-equivalent legal effect
- Both are verifiable by any third party via the [Doc Validator](/sealing/verification) or standard PDF signature validation in tools like Adobe Acrobat
